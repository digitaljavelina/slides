03 AI foundations
Foundations · since the 1950s

AI is not new — three forces finally converged.

Force 01 · Compute

GPUs at scale

HPC convergence with NSF cyberinfrastructure makes deep training tractable on the data sizes safety pharmacology produces.

Force 02 · Data

Vast curated archives

Chemistry libraries, ECG repositories, whole-slide pathology archives — sufficient signal for modern training regimes.

Force 03 · Algorithms

Deep learning matures

Graph neural networks, transformers, foundation models — newly capable of integrating heterogeneous biomedical signals.

09 Challenges
What still has to be solved

The community's caution is appropriate, not obstructionist.

Constraint 01

Data quality & availability

Domain-specific datasets are small, heterogeneous, and biased by historical screening practices.

Constraint 02

Validation gaps

Few prospective studies; most evidence is retrospective and limited to internal benchmarks.

Constraint 03

Interpretability

Explainable AI remains a regulatory expectation — opaque predictions don't carry weight in safety review.

Constraint 04

Implementation

Regulatory acceptance, governance, and integration with established study workflows lag behind technical capability.

10 Where the field is heading
Future directions
Direction 01

Multimodal foundation models

Joint reasoning over chemical structure, assay readouts, imaging, and textual reports.

Direction 02

Federated learning

Cross-pharma model training without sharing proprietary data — e.g. MELLODDY.

Direction 03

Organ-on-chip × AI

Microphysiological systems integrated with AI-driven readouts, advancing non-animal evaluation.
